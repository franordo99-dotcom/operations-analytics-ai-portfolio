# Methodology

This portfolio uses a business-first approach to operations analytics, automation, and applied AI.

## 1. Start With The Business Problem

The first question is not which tool to use. The first question is what operational problem needs to be solved: manual work, weak traceability, data quality issues, duplicate risk, reporting delays, or process friction.

## 2. Map The Workflow

Before automating, the process is broken down into inputs, decisions, handoffs, validations, outputs, and control points.

This helps separate what can be automated from what should remain under human review.

## 3. Identify Data And Control Points

Operational workflows usually fail at the edges: incomplete inputs, inconsistent formats, duplicated records, unclear ownership, or weak audit trails.

The design should make those points visible.

## 4. Design The Automation Carefully

Automation should reduce manual effort without removing accountability. In ERP-related workflows, this usually means staging data, validating it, and creating draft records before any final impact.

## 5. Keep Humans In The Loop

Applied AI and automation are most useful when they prepare structured information for human review. The goal is not to replace operational judgment. The goal is to make review faster, safer, and more traceable.

## 6. Validate And Audit

A useful workflow includes validation before system write and audit after system write. This improves trust and makes the process easier to scale.

## 7. Publish With Privacy First

Public case studies are curated. They do not include raw private data, internal documents, credentials, logs, or business-sensitive details.
